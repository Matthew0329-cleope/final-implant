About the Ultrasound Brain Implant Trial

This NHS trial is investigating a cutting-edge brain-computer interface (BCI) developed by Forest Neurotech. The device, implanted beneath the skull but outside the brain, maps neural activity and delivers targeted ultrasound pulses to activate specific neuron clusters. The trial seeks to determine the safety and tolerability of this technology in approximately 30 patients.

The research is funded by the UK's Advanced Research and Invention Agency (ARIA) and involves experts like Aimun Jamjoom and Elsa Fouragnan. The ultimate goal is to revolutionize the treatment of various neurological and psychiatric conditions by rebalancing disrupted brain activity patterns.
